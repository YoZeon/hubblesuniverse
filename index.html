What is Hubble's Law?

The principle that describes how fast galaxies move away from us.

The Discovery

In 1929, Edwin Hubble found that distant galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they move.

Hubble's Law: v = H₀ × d

Where v is velocity, H₀ is Hubble's constant, and d is distance.

Why It Matters

The discovery showed the universe expands everywhere, like an inflating balloon, supporting the Big Bang theory.

Hubble's Law helps measure distances to far-off galaxies and understand the universe's history and future.

The Evidence

Multiple lines of evidence support the expanding universe.

Redshift

Light from receding galaxies is stretched to longer (redder) wavelengths. Faster recession → more redshift.

Big Bang

If galaxies are moving apart now, they were closer in the past — leading to a hot, dense beginning.

CMB

The Cosmic Microwave Background is the afterglow of the Big Bang, detected in all directions.

Key Figures

Minds that transformed our understanding of the universe.

Edwin Hubble

1889–1953

American astronomer who provided evidence the universe expands, using the 100-inch Mount Wilson telescope.

His 1929 observations led to Hubble's Law. The Hubble Space Telescope honors his legacy.

Key Discovery: Expanding Universe

Georges Lemaître

1894–1966

Belgian physicist who proposed the “primeval atom” — the early idea of the Big Bang — deriving expansion from Einstein's equations.

Hubble's later observations supported his theoretical insight.

Key Discovery: Big Bang Theory

Modern Discoveries

Recent breakthroughs continue to expand our cosmic knowledge.

WMAP Survey

Detailed CMB maps refined the universe's age to ~13.8 billion years.

Dark Energy

A mysterious force discovered in 1998 causing accelerated expansion.

Precision Cosmology
